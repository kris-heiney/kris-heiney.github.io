RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

Alexander von Humboldt Fellow Mar 2024 – present Sprekeler Lab, Technical University of Berlin Control group, Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge Supervisors: Prof. Henning Sprekeler, Prof. Timothy O’Leary

I am using models to investigate the role of inhibitory neurons in guiding drifting neural representations. How does inhibition constrain drift and maintain the neural code?

Visiting researcher Feb–Jul 2022, Apr–Jun 2025
Control group, Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge
Supervisors: Prof. Timothy O’Leary, Dr. Mónika Józsa, Dr. Michael E. Rule

I studied the relationship between noise correlations and representational drift: can a neuron’s level of population-wide redundancy or synergy inform us of how likely it is to drift?

PhD Fellow Oct 2018 – Jun 2023
Living Technology Lab, Oslo Metropolitan University
SOCRATES: Self-Organizing Computational substRATES, funded by the Research Council of Norway
Supervisor: Prof. Stefano Nichele

The focus of my doctoral thesis work was to understand neural computation across scales: how do interactions among individual neurons lead to population-level information representation and computation? I approached this question through a combination of data analysis and data-driven modeling, with an eye toward how my findings can inform the design of computing systems

Master-level researcher Feb 2017 – Jun 2018
Neuroengineering and Computational Neuroscience group, i3S
Supervisor: Dr. Paulo Aguiar

I created and published an advanced computational tool based in MATLAB for the analysis of electrophysiological signals recorded from neurons cultured on microelectrode arrays interfaced with microfluidic chambers
